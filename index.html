Projects

  • Dijitali Maths Club: Empowering students with modern math and digital tools. In Kenya, digital literacy is becoming increasingly essential for academic and professional success, yet many students in underserved communities face limited access to digital resources and skills training. According to the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS), only about 22% of households in Kenya have access to the internet at home, with this percentage significantly lower in rural and low-income areas. Furthermore, the World Bank highlights that digital skills are crucial for over 90% of jobs worldwide, making it critical for young people to develop these competencies early. In Kayole 1 Comprehensive School, the average score in the 2024 mathematics end term assessments study remains below the desired proficiency levels, with many students struggling in foundational areas like data handling, logic, and applied math concepts that are critical for digital literacy. These challenges limit students' readiness for higher education and their ability to participate in a digital economy.

    Solution:The Digitali Math Club aims to bridge the digital literacy and math skills gap among students in underserved communities by providing a structured program that integrates foundational digital and mathematical concepts. Through this project, students in the Junior Secondary Section receive hands-on training in essential skills like data handling, logical reasoning, and applied math using digital tools such as Wayground, programming and AI resources. By leveraging accessible technology, the Digitali Maths Club fosters an engaging learning environment where students can improve their math proficiency and develop critical digital skills, essential for their academic advancement and future career opportunities. The program includes interactive workshops, mentorship sessions, and access to online resources, ensuring that students gain practical, real-world experience in navigating digital platforms. By building both math and digital literacy competencies, the Digitali Maths Club not only boosts students' readiness for higher education but also equips them with the tools needed to participate actively in a digital economy. This initiative addresses the core educational barriers identified at Kayole 1 Comprehensive School and empowers students to succeed in a technology-driven world.
